English Grammar

Parts of Speech

  • Noun — a person, place, thing, or idea (dog, city, love)
  • Pronoun — replaces a noun (he, she, it, they)
  • Verb — an action or state (run, is, become)
  • Adjective — describes a noun (beautiful, tall, quick)
  • Adverb — describes a verb, adjective, or other adverb (quickly, very)
  • Preposition — shows relationship (in, on, at, between)
  • Conjunction — connects words or clauses (and, but, because)
  • Interjection — expresses emotion (wow, ouch, hey)

Sentence Structure

A standard English sentence follows: Subject + Verb + Object

Example: "She (subject) reads (verb) books (object)."

Sentences can be simple (one clause), compound (two independent clauses joined by a conjunction), or complex (one independent + one dependent clause).
