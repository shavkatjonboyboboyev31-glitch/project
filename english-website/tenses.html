English Tenses

There are 12 tenses in English, formed from three time frames (past, present, future) and four aspects (simple, continuous, perfect, perfect continuous).

Present Tenses

TenseExampleUse
Present SimpleI walk to school.Habits, facts, routines
Present ContinuousI am walking now.Actions happening now
Present PerfectI have walked there.Past actions with present relevance
Present Perfect ContinuousI have been walking for an hour.Actions that started in the past and continue

Past Tenses

TenseExampleUse
Past SimpleI walked yesterday.Completed past actions
Past ContinuousI was walking when it rained.Ongoing past actions interrupted
Past PerfectI had walked before you called.Past action before another past action
Past Perfect ContinuousI had been walking for two hours.Duration of a past action before another

Future Tenses

TenseExampleUse
Future SimpleI will walk tomorrow.Predictions, promises, spontaneous decisions
Future ContinuousI will be walking at 8 PM.Actions in progress at a future time
Future PerfectI will have walked by noon.Actions completed before a future time
Future Perfect ContinuousI will have been walking for three hours.Duration of an action up to a future time
